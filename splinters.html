Splinter and Workshop sessions

As an experiment for this year, the AstroNWxSW 2018 SOC invites the community to propose splinter sessions or workshops for the second day (Nov 4) of the meeting. While the meeting itself will cover a broad range of topics, the goal of the splinter sessions and workshops is to take advantage of having a critical mass of astronomers to get together and achieve a focussed goal.

In 2018, the following splinter sessions will run on Sunday afternoon.

Planetary Science Splinter Session

Organized by Melissa Rice (WWU), Darci Snowden (CWU), Matthew Hedman (Idaho), Jason Barnes (Idaho)
This session will bring together researchers, educators, students and other professionals with interests in planetary science to discuss our current projects and opportunities for future collaborations in the NWxSW region. Discussion topics may include: specific subfields and research programs; teaching planetary science; student research opportunities; public engagement efforts; career pathways in academia and industry; ways to foster collaborations across institutions.

If you are interested in this splinter session, please let the organizers know via this Google Form by October 26.

Github Workshop

Organized by Jim Davenport
In this workshop, we will introduce Github and learn core skills necessary for an effective Github workflow. This will be a hands-on tutorial where we will work in real-time to create a simple code repository and learn how to clone a repository, make a branch, commit and push our changes, and make/merge pull requests. We will also cover useful tools such as issue tracking. This workshop is intended for those completely new to github/version control as well as those who have basic experience but want to learn some best practices.
