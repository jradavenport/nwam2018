Meeting format

Join us on November 3rd and 4th for Astronomy Northwest by Southwest 2018 (Astro NWxSW 2018; formerly Northwest Astronomy Meeting). This two-day meeting, held in Vancouver, BC, seeks to bring together astronomers from around Cascadia to promote discussions, highlight the wide range of science conducted in the region, and to help foster local science networks. A tentative program is provided below, consisting of talks and posters as well as splinter/workshop sessions.

Splinter and Workshop sessions

As an experiment for this year, the NWAM2018 SOC invites the community to propose splinter sessions or workshops for the second day (Nov 4) of the meeting. While the meeting itself will cover a broad range of topics, the goal of the splinter sessions and workshops is to take advantage of having a critical mass of astronomers to get together and achieve a focussed goal.

Splinter sessions and workshops can be proposed by one person or a group of people. We anticipate having 2-3 splinters/workshops, scheduled for approximately 2-3 hours on Sunday afternoon. The focused goal of a splinter or workshop could be topical (e.g. a series of talks on a specific shared interest) or to provide a hands-on workshop for a specific skill (e.g. a tutorial on collaborative scientific software development with Github).

Please submit your proposal by September 10 using this Google Form. If there is enough interest, the SOC will select and schedule splinter sessions, otherwise, we will continue the plenary talk format from Saturday. You can also take a look at this example workshop session proposal.

We plan to announce accepted sessions by September 24 will put splinter session information on this website as soon as possible.
